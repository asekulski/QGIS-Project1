Analysis Methodology

This project applies geospatial analysis techniques to assess Urban Heat Island (UHI) intensity and its intersection with socioeconomic vulnerability across a metropolitan study area.

Data Layers

analysis_grid — 400-cell grid (500m resolution) with surface temperature, NDVI, impervious surface %, land use classification, population density, median income, tree canopy coverage, and a composite vulnerability index.

temperature_sensors — 300 sensor readings (ground, mobile, rooftop, satellite-derived) captured during peak afternoon hours on a summer day.

green_spaces — 45 parks with area, tree density, cooling effect radius, and accessibility scoring.

buildings — 500 footprints with height, material, albedo, green roof status, and energy rating.

transit_stops — 80 stops with ridership, shade infrastructure, and heat exposure scores.

Analysis Pipeline

Heat-Equity Index (HEI) — Composite score: 35% surface temperature + 25% income disparity + 20% canopy deficit + 20% vulnerability index. Identifies areas where heat burden and social vulnerability converge.

IDW Interpolation — Inverse Distance Weighted interpolation of sensor readings produces a continuous heat surface raster (QGIS Processing).

Spatial Autocorrelation — Global Moran's I computed for temperature and NDVI to quantify clustering patterns.

ML Feature Engineering — Spatial lag, local density statistics, and accessibility metrics exported as a training-ready CSV for predictive modeling.

Tools & Technologies

QGIS 3.34+ • Python (PyQGIS) • QGIS Processing Framework • Leaflet.js • GeoJSON
